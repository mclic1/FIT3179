Population density by country

Equal Earth Projection

The map to the right is a an equal earth projection of the world map, that demonstrates the population density of a country. The darker the country, the more dense it is.

Visually, the countries that seem most densely populated are Bangladesh, India, South Korea and perhaps the Netherlands.

Comparing Population with Land Size

In the visualisation to the left, we can see the population of each country compared with its land size; both variables have been transformed using the logarithm function. The population density is indicated by the size of the circle - the larger the circle, the more dense the country is.

This map can be filtered by continent using the dropdown in the bottom left corner, and can be zoomed in for greater precision.

The most notable mentions are Greenland, which has a large land mass, yet small population. It's population density is only 0.14 people per square kilometer. One can compare this with India, whose population density is over 450 people per square kilometer!
